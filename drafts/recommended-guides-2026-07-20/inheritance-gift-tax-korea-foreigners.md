### Map people, residence, and assets

Create a chart showing the deceased or donor, every heir or recipient, residence and domicile at the relevant date, nationality, and each asset's location. Do not begin with nationality as the deciding factor.

### Identify the transfer

Gifts can include cash, property, shares, debt forgiveness, free use, or a transfer for less than adequate value. An inheritance inventory should include liabilities and prior transfers that may affect the estate calculation.

### Value assets under Korean rules

Bank balances are usually straightforward, while real estate, private-company shares, trusts, and foreign assets can require specialized valuation. Save exchange rates, appraisals, transaction evidence, and ownership documents.

### Protect the deadline

The Korean filing clock can run while foreign probate, appraisal, or tax proceedings remain open. Obtain Korean advice early, estimate missing values where legally permitted, and preserve the ability to amend if later information changes.
