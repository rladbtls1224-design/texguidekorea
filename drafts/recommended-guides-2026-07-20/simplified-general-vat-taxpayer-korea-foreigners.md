### Confirm classification before quoting prices

Read the business registration certificate and Hometax profile. Determine whether quoted prices include VAT and whether business customers expect a tax invoice. Correcting a contract after registration is harder than pricing correctly from the start.

### Compare the calculation mechanics

A general taxpayer normally calculates output VAT on taxable sales and subtracts eligible input VAT supported by proper evidence. A simplified taxpayer uses special rules based on sales and the relevant business category. Refund, credit, and invoice outcomes can differ.

### Review eligibility beyond revenue

Industry exclusions, business location, related businesses, prior activity, and other statutory facts can matter in addition to sales. Do not rely on a single threshold quoted without a tax year.

### Plan for transition

If growth is likely, keep purchase and sales evidence at the standard needed for general VAT. Review open contracts, invoice wording, cash flow, and prices before a conversion date.
