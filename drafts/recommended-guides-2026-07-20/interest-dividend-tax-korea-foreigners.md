### Classify the taxpayer and the payment

Determine tax residence for the payment period, the source country, whether the payment is interest or a dividend, and whether the recipient is the beneficial owner. The same bank or broker statement can lead to different treatment for a Korean resident and a treaty-country non-resident.

### Reconcile withholding

Record gross income and tax withheld rather than only the net cash received. Check whether withholding is final, creditable against a return, or subject to a treaty refund or adjustment.

### Review annual financial income

Korean residents with total interest and dividend income above the applicable annual threshold may need to consider aggregation with other global income. Special rules can apply to particular dividends, so use the income year and product details.

### Include cross-border relief

For overseas income, confirm whether it belongs in the Korean tax base and whether qualifying foreign tax is creditable. For Korean-source payments to a non-resident, review the treaty rate and required claim procedure before payment where possible.
