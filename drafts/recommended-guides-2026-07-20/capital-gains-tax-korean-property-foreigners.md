### Confirm the taxable event and seller status

Identify the legal transfer date, property type, ownership percentage, and whether the seller is a Korean tax resident. Review any treaty, but expect Korea to retain taxing rights over gains from Korean real estate in many cases.

### Reconstruct basis and costs

Collect the original purchase contract, acquisition tax, registration and legal fees, brokerage, and supported capital improvements. Routine repairs, financing costs, and unsupported cash payments should not be assumed deductible.

### Test special rules separately

Principal-home exemptions, multiple-home rules, holding-period deductions, unregistered assets, and non-resident limitations can materially change the result. Do not estimate tax using only the headline progressive rate.

### Prepare before closing

The buyer, escrow professional, or bank may request tax documents, especially when the seller is overseas. Confirm withholding, remittance, certificate, and filing requirements before the final settlement, then protect the two-month preliminary return deadline.
