### Why visa type matters, but does not answer everything

Foreigners in Korea often hear that tax documents are needed because of a visa. That can be true, but it is not the whole picture. A visa type may explain why an office asks for income proof, while the actual tax document depends on what income was reported, which year is being checked, and what the official checklist says.

For example, an employee, student with part-time income, spouse visa holder, long-term resident, permanent resident applicant, or specialized worker may all face different document requests. The safest starting point is to separate two questions: "Do I have a tax filing or reporting issue?" and "What document is the office asking for?"

### Documents people often confuse

A certificate of income is not the same as a tax payment certificate. A withholding tax receipt is also different because it is usually tied to a payer or employer. Immigration, schools, banks, and employers may use similar English descriptions, so try to get the Korean document name before you issue anything.

If a document comes from Hometax or Government24, it usually depends on official records. If your employer or payer did not report income, or if you select the wrong year, the certificate may look incomplete even if you personally received money.

### How to prepare before a visa appointment

Start with the official checklist or request message. Write down the document name, year, issue date requirement, and whether online certificates are accepted. Then check Hometax early enough to fix login problems, missing income, or payer-record questions before the appointment.

If your income history is complicated, collect supporting records such as contracts, payslips, withholding receipts, business payment statements, and employer certificates. These may not replace official tax certificates, but they can help you understand what should appear in your tax records.
