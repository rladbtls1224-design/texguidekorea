### Read the bracket table correctly

Korea's progressive schedule uses marginal rates. For income attributable to 2025, the NTS table shows 6% up to KRW 14 million, 15% from above KRW 14 million to KRW 50 million, 24% to KRW 88 million, 35% to KRW 150 million, 38% to KRW 300 million, 40% to KRW 500 million, 42% to KRW 1 billion, and 45% above KRW 1 billion.

The progressive deduction in the official table is a shortcut for calculating the same marginal result. It does not create an extra tax benefit.

### Start from the tax base, not gross pay

An employee's gross salary is adjusted under the wage-income rules and permitted deductions. A freelancer generally starts from business revenue and subtracts recognized necessary expenses before other parts of the calculation. The resulting tax base is what meets the bracket table.

### Reconcile withholding and final tax

Employers and payers may withhold tax during the year. The final calculation can still change through year-end settlement or a May return. A refund means prior tax exceeded the final result; an additional payment means it was insufficient.

### Compare special rules carefully

Some eligible foreign workers can choose a special flat-tax method. Because that method can replace ordinary deductions, exemptions, and credits, compare both results using the same income year and verified eligibility before choosing.
