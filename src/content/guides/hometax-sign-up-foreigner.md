### Why does Hometax access matter?

Hometax is the main online tax portal used for many Korean tax tasks. For foreigners, it can be needed for income tax filing, refund checks, tax payment, certificates of income, tax payment certificates, and reported income review. Even if an employer handles year-end settlement, you may still need Hometax later for documents.

The hardest part is often not the tax concept but login setup. Names, registration numbers, mobile phone identity records, and authentication methods must line up. If one detail differs, the system may reject access or fail verification.

### What should you prepare before signing up?

Prepare your foreigner registration information, the spelling of your name as used by Korean institutions, and any authentication options available to you. If you have a Korean mobile phone under your own name, it may help with verification. If not, check current Hometax guidance or ask the tax office what alternatives are available.

| Item | Why it helps |
| --- | --- |
| Foreigner registration details | Used for identity matching |
| Name spelling | Korean systems can be sensitive to spacing and order |
| Authentication method | Needed for login or certificate issuance |
| Contact details | May be used for alerts or verification |

### What should you do after creating access?

After you can log in, confirm that basic records appear as expected. Do not change or submit tax data unless you understand the purpose of the screen. If your goal is filing, continue to the [Hometax filing procedure](/procedures/how-to-file-income-tax-on-hometax/). If your goal is a document, use the [income certificate procedure](/procedures/how-to-get-income-certificate/).

Menu names and screen layouts may change. Always check official Hometax or National Tax Service information before relying on any navigation path.
