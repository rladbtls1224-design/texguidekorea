### Do not ignore a missed filing deadline

Missing Korea's May income tax filing period can feel embarrassing, but the practical answer is simple: check it now. Some foreigners miss filing because they thought 3.3% withholding completed the process. Others thought employer year-end settlement covered side income. Some left Korea and lost easy access to Hometax.

The right next step depends on your facts. You may owe additional tax, you may have overpaid through withholding, or you may find that the income was handled differently than you expected. Do not make the decision from memory alone. Use records.

### Step 1: Check whether filing may have been needed

Start with your income type. Salary from one employer may have been handled through year-end settlement. Freelance income, business-type income, multiple employers, side income, or mixed income may require a closer review.

### Step 2: Gather records before filing late

Collect withholding receipts, payslips, invoices, payment statements, contracts, and bank deposits. Then compare those records with Hometax. If Hometax is missing income, ask the payer whether and how it was reported.

This matters because filing late with incomplete data can create a second problem. You want to understand the income year, income category, payer, tax withheld, and whether any tax notices already exist.

### Step 3: Ask the right channel

Use official NTS or Hometax information for general procedures. For personal calculations, penalties, refunds, or correction filings, a qualified tax professional may be safer. If you are outside Korea, contact options and authentication can be harder, so start earlier rather than waiting for another deadline.

### What not to do

Do not assume the issue is small because the income was small. Do not assume 3.3% withholding guarantees a refund. Do not pay a tax amount from an unofficial message without verifying it through official channels. And do not wait until next May if the missed filing relates to a previous tax year.
