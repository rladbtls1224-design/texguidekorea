### Do not start with the percentage

Start with the taxpayer and business: identify the industry code, whether the business is new, current and prior-year revenue, and the applicable bookkeeping obligation. Only then determine whether an official expense-rate method is available.

### Understand the formula difference

The simple expense rate estimates a broader portion of expenses through an official rate. The standard expense rate generally separates specified major costs, such as purchase costs, rent, and payroll, from a smaller rate applied to the remaining base. Exact treatment must follow current official instructions.

### Compare with actual books

An expense-rate result can be higher or lower than actual net income. If actual costs are substantial and well documented, bookkeeping may produce a more accurate result. Conversely, missing records can limit separately recognized costs.

### Verify the current-year data

Rates, thresholds, industry codes, and filing screens can change. Use the official table for the income year being filed and save the basis for the method chosen.
