### Why paying from abroad can be harder

Korean tax payment is often easiest while you still have Korean phone verification, a Korean bank account, and access to local support. Once you are abroad, authentication, banking, and communication with former employers or payers can become more difficult.

The first step is to identify what you are trying to pay. Is it national income tax, local income tax, a filing result, or a notice? The answer affects the official service you should use.

### What should you check before payment?

Review the tax year, amount, taxpayer name, and tax type. If the notice came by email or message, verify it through official channels before paying. Keep a PDF, screenshot, or receipt after payment.

| Problem | Possible next step |
| --- | --- |
| Hometax login fails | Check official support or tax office contact |
| Korean bank account closed | Ask official channels about payment options |
| Local tax appears unpaid | Check Wetax or official local tax service |

### What should you do before leaving Korea?

If possible, set up Hometax, download important certificates, confirm year-end settlement or filing status, and keep payer contact details before departure. This can save a lot of friction later.
