### Why F-2-7 income proof needs early checking

F-2-7 visa paperwork can feel more stressful than ordinary tax document requests because income, tax records, employment status, and immigration timing may all matter at once. The important first step is to avoid guessing from English summaries. Ask for the current official checklist and confirm the Korean document names, the tax year, the issue date requirement, and whether online certificates are accepted.

Tax records are not created just because money entered your bank account. Official Korean tax certificates usually depend on income reported by an employer, academy, company, platform, or payer. If income was reported late, reported under different identity details, or not reported at all, your certificate may look lower than expected.

### Employees, freelancers, and mixed income

Employees usually start with employer documents, salary withholding records, and year-end settlement results. If you changed jobs during the year, ask each employer what was reported and keep the wage and salary income withholding receipt.

Freelancers should check payer records more carefully. If a company withheld 3.3% from your payment, that does not automatically mean the income appears correctly on every certificate. Ask for a withholding receipt or payment statement and compare the payer name, income amount, tax year, and registration details.

If you have both salary and side income, do not assume employer year-end settlement covered everything. You may need to review whether separate global income tax filing applies and whether all income is visible before relying on a certificate for immigration paperwork.

### Practical preparation order

1. Get the current document list from official immigration guidance or the office handling your case.
2. Write down the Korean document name, year, and issue date rule.
3. Check Hometax login before your appointment is close.
4. Issue a sample certificate if possible and compare it with your own records.
5. Ask employers or payers for missing withholding receipts or payment statements.
6. If the record is wrong, ask the payer, Hometax support, or a tax office about correction steps.

### When to get professional help

Ask for help if your income record affects visa eligibility, if income is missing from Hometax, if you have several employers or payers, or if overseas income and Korean tax residency are involved. Immigration and tax questions can overlap, but they are not the same problem. A tax professional can help you understand the tax record, while the immigration office decides what documents it will accept.
