### Reconcile all revenue first

Build a payer-by-payer revenue list from contracts, bank deposits, platform statements, cash receipts, and Hometax data. Investigate differences instead of reporting only the amount currently visible online.

### Apply the business-purpose test

For each cost, write down how it helped produce or maintain business income. Common categories can include professional software, platform fees, supplies, business communications, advertising, professional services, and equipment. The label is less important than the facts and evidence.

### Handle mixed use consistently

Phone, internet, workspace, vehicle, and travel costs can include personal use. Use a reasonable and documented business allocation rather than claiming 100% by default. Apply the same method consistently.

### Match the filing method

Actual bookkeeping, simple books, and official expense-rate methods do not produce the same calculation. Determine the method and record-keeping obligation for the relevant industry, revenue level, and tax year before finalizing deductions.
