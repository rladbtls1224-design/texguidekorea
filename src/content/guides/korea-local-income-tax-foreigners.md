### What is local income tax?

Local income tax is connected to income tax, but it can appear separately in notices, payment screens, and certificates. For foreigners, this can be confusing because the main tax filing conversation often focuses on Hometax and the National Tax Service. However, local tax may still need to be checked when paying, confirming status, or preparing documents.

If you are not sure what a notice means, identify the tax type first. A payment amount may relate to national tax, local income tax, or another local tax item.

### How should foreigners handle payment checks?

Before paying, confirm the tax year, payer name, amount, and whether the payment is national or local. Save all payment records. If the local tax service uses a separate route, do not assume Hometax alone shows everything.

| Item | What to check |
| --- | --- |
| Tax type | National tax, local income tax, or other local tax |
| Payment route | Hometax, Wetax, bank, or official payment service |
| Confirmation | Receipt, payment date, amount, and tax year |

### When should you ask for help?

Ask for help if the amount does not match your filing result, if you paid one tax but another remains unpaid, or if you need proof for visa or business purposes. Use the [online payment procedure](/procedures/how-to-pay-korean-tax-online/) as a practical checklist.
