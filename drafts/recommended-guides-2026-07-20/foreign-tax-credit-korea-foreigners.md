### Confirm that the same income is in the Korean return

A foreign tax credit is designed to relieve overlapping income tax. Start by matching the foreign tax to the exact income item, country, and tax year included in Korea. Do not claim a payment merely because it appeared on a foreign account statement.

### Identify qualifying foreign income tax

The NTS describes qualifying foreign income taxes and excludes penalty tax and additional charges. Property taxes, social insurance, consumption taxes, and other levies should not be assumed to qualify simply because they were paid overseas.

### Apply the Korean ceiling

The credit is limited by the portion of Korean calculated tax attributable to foreign-source income under the statutory formula. When several countries or income categories are involved, allocation can be complex. An excess foreign payment is not automatically refundable by Korea.

### Build an evidence package

Keep the foreign return, assessment, payment receipt, income statement, translation if needed, and exchange-rate record. If foreign tax is assessed or refunded later, ask whether the Korean return requires an amendment.
